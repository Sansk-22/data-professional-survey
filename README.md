# Data Professional Survey Breakdown (Power BI)

An interactive Power BI dashboard analysing survey responses from 630 data professionals globally — covering salary trends, job satisfaction, programming language preferences, and demographic insights.

---

## Tools & Technologies
- **Power BI** — data cleaning (Power Query), DAX measures, interactive dashboard

---

## Dataset
**Source:** Alex the Analyst's Data Professional Survey dataset (publicly available)  
**Size:** 630 survey respondents across multiple countries and job roles

---

## Dashboard Visuals

- **Survey takers by country** — treemap showing US and India as dominant regions
- **Average salary by job title** — horizontal bar chart across 7 roles
- **Average salary by gender** — donut chart comparing male vs female earnings
- **Favourite programming language by job title** — stacked bar chart
- **Happiness Index: Work/Life Balance** — gauge chart (5.74 / 10)
- **Happiness Index: Salary** — gauge chart (4.27 / 10)
- **KPI cards** — total respondents (630) and average age (29.87)

---

## Key Findings

- **630 data professionals** surveyed with an average age of **29.87 years**
- **Data Scientists earn the highest average salary** (~$93K), followed by Data Engineers (~$65K)
- **Salary satisfaction is low** — average happiness score of just **4.27/10**
- **Work/life balance is rated better** at **5.74/10** but still below average
- **Python dominates** as the favourite programming language across all job titles
- **Gender pay gap exists** — males earn ~$55K vs females ~$53K on average
- **US and India** account for the largest share of survey respondents

---

## How to Open
- Download `projectbi.pbix` and open in **Power BI Desktop**
- All data is embedded — no external data source connection needed
